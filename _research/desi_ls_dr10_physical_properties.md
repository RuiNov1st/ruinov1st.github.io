Galaxy physical properties, including star formation rate (SFR), stellar mass, and gas-phase metallicity, are essential for population studies and galaxy evolution analyses. However, deriving these quantities for very large photometric surveys remains challenging because spectroscopy is limited and traditional spectral energy distribution fitting is computationally expensive at survey scale.

In this work, we developed a multimodal deep learning model that combines optical imaging with photometric catalog features to estimate SFR, stellar mass, and oxygen abundance for low-redshift galaxies.

### Key Contributions

**1. Multimodal Physical Property Estimation**

The model integrates image-based spatial information with catalog-level photometric features, using complementary constraints from morphology, surface brightness, and broadband colors.

**2. Large-scale DESI LS DR10 Catalog**

Applied to DESI Legacy Imaging Surveys DR10, the model produces a value-added catalog of physical property estimates for approximately 547 million galaxies with redshifts z <= 0.5.

**3. Resource for Statistical Galaxy Studies**

Validation against independent catalogs and scaling relations shows that the catalog captures dominant astrophysical trends for ensemble studies of galaxies in the local Universe.
